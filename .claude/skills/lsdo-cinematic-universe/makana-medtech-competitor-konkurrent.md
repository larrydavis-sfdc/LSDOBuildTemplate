# Konkurrent Medical

Part of the [Makana MedTech Cinematic Universe](makana-medtech-cinematic-universe.md).

Konkurrent Medical is the primary competitor in LSDO MedTech demos. A Nordic-headquartered medical device company competing across [Makana's](makana-life-sciences.md) surgical robotics, interventional vascular, and cardiac product lines. Konkurrent's products use External IDs in the `LS.MT.Product2.101`–`LS.MT.Product2.159` range (see [Makana products](makana-medtech-products.md) for the `001`–`099` range).

When building competitive scenarios, pair Konkurrent products with [Makana personas](makana-medtech-personas.md) (especially [Carmen Central](makana-medtech-personas.md#Carmen%20Central%20—%20MedTech%20Sales%20Rep) and [Sasha Sato](makana-medtech-personas.md#Sasha%20Sato%20—%20MedTech%20Clinical%20Specialist)) and customer personas at [accounts](makana-accounts.md) like [United Hospital](makana-accounts.md#United%20Hospital%20(Facility)).

## Fjord Surgical Robot

Konkurrent's flagship surgical robotics platform competing directly with the [Aurora Surgical Robot](makana-medtech-products.md#Aurora%20Surgical%20Robot). Sold as a configured capital system with instruments and service contracts. Fjord is positioned as the established, surgeon-familiar platform — emphasizing its large global installed base and bundled service model. Aurora has disrupted the market by leading with AI-powered planning and real-time guidance, which is where Makana wins the innovation narrative.

### Competitive Narrative

Use these talking points when building demo stories where Makana competes against Konkurrent for surgical robotics deals. [Sales personas](makana-medtech-personas.md) like [Carmen Central](makana-medtech-personas.md#Carmen%20Central%20—%20MedTech%20Sales%20Rep) (MedTech Sales Rep) and [Ezra Executive](makana-medtech-personas.md#Ezra%20Executive%20—%20VP%20of%20Sales) (VP of Sales) would use these in their workflows.

| Dimension | Fjord (Konkurrent) | Aurora (Makana) |
|-----------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Positioning | "The trusted platform surgeons know" | "AI-powered precision surgery — smarter outcomes" |
| Key strength | Large global installed base (~3,200 systems), surgeon familiarity, bundled 3-year service | Integrated AI surgical planning, real-time intraoperative guidance overlays, Aurora Halo analytics ecosystem |
| Weakness to exploit | Aging platform architecture; no native AI; limited analytics; higher instrument cost per procedure | Newer platform, still building installed base in some markets |
| Installed base | ~3,200 systems globally (dominant in US, but aging) | ~800 systems globally (rapidly growing, AI-differentiated) |
| Service model | Bundled 3-year service included in purchase | Annual maintenance sold separately |
| Portal | Fjord Insight (basic procedure data, no AI recommendations) | Aurora Halo (AI-powered pre-op planning, real-time guidance, physician-specific benchmarking) |
| Training | In-person proctoring + online certification | Aurora Academy VR simulator + AI-assisted simulation |
| Price position | Lower system price; higher instrument cost per procedure | Higher system price (AI premium justified); lower instrument cost per procedure |

### Portal

**Fjord Insight** — Konkurrent's physician data portal. Provides basic procedure metrics and historical case data. Does not offer AI-generated planning recommendations or real-time intraoperative guidance. When hospitals compare Aurora Halo vs Fjord Insight, Aurora wins on analytics sophistication. Use in demo stories where the physician analytics gap is a deal factor.

### Vann PTA Balloon Catheters

Competes with [Makana's Stratus and Cyclone PTA Balloon Catheters](makana-medtech-products.md#PTA%20Balloon%20Catheters). Vann catheters are positioned on superior crossing profile for tight lesions, whereas Makana positions Stratus on reliability and Cyclone on balloon compliance.

Available in same size configurations as Stratus/Cyclone (6 French, balloon lengths 40-100mm, catheter lengths 40-135cm). ## Demo Story Scenarios

Ready-to-use competitive scenarios for demos. Each maps to specific Salesforce features. [Personas](makana-medtech-personas.md) involved are noted where applicable.

### Scenario: Surgical Robot Displacement

**Story:** Abbott Northwestern Hospital has 2 Fjord systems installed (see `LS.MT.Asset.007`). Dr. James Park is the primary user. Contract renewal in 6 months. [Carmen Central](makana-medtech-personas.md#Carmen%20Central%20—%20MedTech%20Sales%20Rep) is positioning Aurora as a replacement based on Aurora's AI-powered surgical planning, superior Halo analytics, and lower instrument cost per procedure. Fjord's age and lack of AI capability are the opening.

**Salesforce features demonstrated:** Opportunity competitor tracking, installed base (competitor Assets), contract renewal pipeline, TCO analysis, competitive battle cards in Sales Cloud, Aurora Halo vs Fjord Insight portal comparison.

**Key records:** Fjord Surgical Robot System (`LS.MT.Product2.101`), Aurora Surgical Robot System (`LS.MT.Product2.024`), Abbott Northwestern Fjord Robot System (`LS.MT.Asset.007`)

### Scenario: Vascular Market Share Battle

**Story:** Large hospital network is standardizing PTA catheters across 12 facilities. Currently split between Vann (7 sites) and Stratus (5 sites). Upcoming RFP for exclusive 3-year supply agreement. [Kimberly Spark](makana-medtech-personas.md#Kimberly%20Spark%20—%20Procurement%20Manager) (Customer Procurement at [United Hospital](makana-accounts.md#United%20Hospital%20(Facility))) is the key decision-maker.

**Salesforce features demonstrated:** Account hierarchy, opportunity splits, multi-site deal tracking, competitive product intelligence, forecast rollup.

**Key records:** Vann PTA Balloon Catheters (`LS.MT.Product2.120`–`.123`), Stratus PTA Balloon Catheters (`LS.MT.Product2.001`–`.012`)

### Scenario: New Technology Evaluation

**Story:** Academic medical center evaluating IVL technology for the first time. Both Nimbus and Strøm are being evaluated. [Rachel Shell](makana-medtech-personas.md#Rachel%20Shell%20—%20Cardiothoracic%20Surgeon) (cardiothoracic surgeon) is the clinical champion. Key decision factors are clinical evidence, training support, and total cost of ownership.

**Salesforce features demonstrated:** Opportunity stage progression, competitor tracking, activity timeline (physician meetings, clinical presentations), content management (clinical papers, ROI calculators).

**Key records:** Strøm IVL Generator (`LS.MT.Product2.130`), Nimbus IVL Generator (`LS.MT.Product2.037`)

### Scenario: Competitive Win-Back

**Story:** Community hospital switched from Aurora to Fjord 2 years ago when Aurora was new and unproven. Aurora has since launched its AI planning suite and Halo portal — the gap has widened dramatically. The hospital is now experiencing Fjord's higher instrument costs and limited analytics. [Carmen Central](makana-medtech-personas.md#Carmen%20Central%20—%20MedTech%20Sales%20Rep) sees an opening to win back the account on the AI and analytics story.

**Salesforce features demonstrated:** Account history, lost opportunity analysis, re-engagement campaigns, Aurora Halo analytics demo, competitive intelligence alerts.

**Key records:** Fjord instruments (`LS.MT.Product2.108`–`.113`), Aurora instruments (`LS.MT.Product2.031`–`.035`)
